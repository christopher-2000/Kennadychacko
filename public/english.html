Welcome to Kennady Chacko Charitable Trust


Kennedy Chacko Charitable Trust
, since 2014, has been working towards helping the disregarded people by the society especially the differently abled ones including the blind, deaf, dumb. We as an organization believe that we are responsible for their safety and welfare. The problems of poverty, illiteracy and homelessness seem to fall hardest on them. Those who struggle to find basic child care and nutrition should be treated accordingly. Shri Kennady Chacko is a very considerate and passionate social worker who spend 27 years of his life providing a helping hand to the needy especially the blind people regarding their food and shelter. He has also been a helping hand to the blind students concerning their education. To reinforce these activities, with God’s grace and with the cooperation of some kind people of the society, the “Kennady Chacko charitable trust” was established in 2014.Simultaneously, as a home for orphans on November 1,2014 “Andharude Aashakendram” was established . Extending the function of the trust, as per the interest of the people of Pathanamthitta and the district government, by the idea “a shelter for the orphans”(orukura) ,
“The Nest”
was registered in 2017.(Reg 24-43)

Listen to this, dear ones. Our "THE NEST" is a humble orphanage operating in Pathanamthitta with the help and cooperation of wellwishers. KENNADY CHACKO CHARITABLE TRUST, which runs this institution, has an NGO license of the central government as well as the 12A, 80G licenses of the state government.- THE NEST orphanage is now home to many blind, cancer patients in different conditions (34 people). We are not asking to be paid for looking after them. The trust is authorized to receive donations from any person in India even on a monthly basis. This organisation does not exclude anyone on the basis of caste, religion, class or politics. Those who work in this trust are all volunteers. Currently we have a Chairman, Manager, Supervisor, Nursing, Kitchen Work and Cleaning and more at the orphanage . Except the chairman(Kennady Chacko), we would like to extend at least a small salary to all those working. No financial support has been received from the government till date. The only support might be the civil ration supplies Rice (1kg-5.65Rs) and wheat (1kg -4.65Rs). Even these are not free We are in a financial crisis now. We need your help. ..Please help. ... a rupee you give will never go waste. Pay at least one rupee from your savings to meet the needs of the needy - this is the mercy you show to the poor.... ... there is no concept of making money here...no one wants to get rich here..

About Us

To Kennady Chacko ,seeing Pathanamthitta as a district where no one has to beg has always been a dream. His made his wish known to the district administration. Pathanamthitta Municipal Corporation, in partnership with District Legal Services Authority and Pathanamthitta Municipal Council, on January 29, 2018 K. Sathyan declared a begging free area. No one should stretch his hands out in front of others for a meal. The institution is ready to feed the hungry and take in the orphans who deserve it.
Projects
• Each year, on the anniversary of the Trust, hundreds of destitute blind families are provided with food and kit.
• The trust is in the process of building a shelter in its own land which is recieved as donation and the construction work is underway.

Aims & Objectives

Mr.Kennedy Chacko works for the disabled and the underprivileged. The Hon'ble Sub-Judge, District Legal Service and Rt. Secretary, Mr. Shri. Jayakrishnan appreciated Mr.Kennady chacko and his trust for their exceptional initiative in erradicating poverty from the district.
Functions of the Trust
1. "THE NEST" is functioning under the Trust. (32 residents on June 30, 2019)
2. Supporting children who are blind, providing them education in collaboration with the Blind School,
3. Conducting organ and awareness-raising classes in various sectors of society.
4. Awareness classes are conducted in conjunction with RTO OFFICE to protect the next generation from road accidents.
5. Need of Police Station and Volunteers | Many patients are referred to a number of charities, especially to the mentally ill.
6. Assistance to patients with visual impairment through treatment.
